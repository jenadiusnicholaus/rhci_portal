How RHCI Works

A transparent process connecting generous donors with patients in need of surgical care

Our Simple Four-Step Process

From identification to treatment, we ensure transparency at every stage

1

Patient Identification

Our medical partners identify patients with congenital surgical conditions who lack financial resources for treatment.

  • Comprehensive medical assessment
  • Financial need verification
  • Treatment plan development
2

Case Verification & Publication

We verify each case and create a profile that donors can browse to understand the medical need and impact potential.

  • Medical documentation review
  • Cost breakdown transparency
  • Expected outcomes explained
3

Donor Contribution

Donors select patients they wish to support and contribute directly toward their surgical expenses.

  • 100% of donations go to patient care
  • Flexible contribution options
  • Secure payment processing
4

Treatment & Follow-up

Once funding is secured, patients receive their life-changing surgery, and donors receive updates on their progress.

  • Treatment documentation
  • Recovery updates
  • Long-term impact reporting

100% Transparency

We believe in complete transparency in every aspect of our work. From the verification of medical needs to the allocation of funds, every step is documented and shared with our donors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our process

Patients are referred to RHCI by our partner hospitals and clinics. Each case undergoes a rigorous verification process, including medical assessment and financial need evaluation. We prioritize cases where surgical intervention will have the most significant life-changing impact.

We maintain direct partnerships with healthcare facilities and make payments directly to them for patient care. We never give cash to patients or families. Every expense is documented with receipts and medical records, which are accessible to the respective donors.

100% of donations designated for specific patients go directly to their medical care. RHCI's operational costs are covered separately through grants and unrestricted donations specifically designated for administrative purposes.

The timeline varies depending on the complexity of the case and how quickly funding is secured. Typically, once a patient is fully funded, surgery is scheduled within 2-4 weeks. The entire process from identification to post-operative follow-up usually takes 3-6 months.

Yes! Donors receive updates through their donor dashboard, including pre-operative preparation, surgery completion notification, and recovery updates. We provide photos (with patient consent) and progress reports while maintaining appropriate medical privacy.

RHCI is a registered non-profit organization in Tanzania. For Tanzanian donors, contributions are tax-deductible. For international donors, tax benefits depend on your country's regulations regarding international charitable contributions.
